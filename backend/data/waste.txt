Waste segregation into biodegradable, non-biodegradable, and hazardous waste is mandatory at the source.

Littering in public places such as roads, parks, and markets is prohibited under municipal laws.

Households must hand over segregated waste only to authorized municipal collectors.

Burning of waste in open areas is prohibited due to health and environmental risks.

Construction and demolition waste must be disposed of at designated collection centers.

Bulk waste generators such as hotels, apartments, and institutions must arrange on-site waste processing.

Non-compliance with waste management rules may lead to fines or suspension of municipal services.

Waste must not be dumped into drains, water bodies, or vacant plots.

Municipal authorities may impose spot fines for littering or illegal disposal of waste.

Plastic waste must be managed as per municipal and environmental regulations.
